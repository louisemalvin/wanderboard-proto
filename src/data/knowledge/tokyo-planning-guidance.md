# Tokyo Planning Guidance — Travel Knowledge

## Pacing Strategies

### Relaxed Pace (2-3 major stops per day)
- Allow 2-3 hours per major neighbourhood plus travel time.
- Pair one major attraction with a leisurely meal, garden visit, or shopping stroll.
- Avoid scheduling more than two areas requiring a train transfer.
- Example relaxed day: Meiji Shrine (morning) + Harajuku/Takeshita (afternoon) + Omotesando dinner.

### Balanced Pace (3-4 stops per day)
- Group 2-3 neighbourhoods that share a train line or are walkable.
- Allow 1.5-2 hours per stop with buffer for transit and meals.
- Consider travel time between areas: Shinjuku to Shibuya is about 7 minutes on JR Yamanote.
- Example balanced day: Tsukiji Outer Market (morning) + Akihabara (midday) + Asakusa (afternoon).

### Packed Pace (5+ stops per day)
- Feasible for energetic travellers who move quickly and plan pre-dawn to late evening.
- Limit geographic spread: stay within 2-3 adjacent wards.
- Packed Shibuya+Harajuku+Shinjuku day example: Meiji Shrine (8am) → Takeshita Street (10am) → Shibuya Crossing/Hachiko (1pm lunch) → Shinjuku Gyoen (3pm) → Golden Gai (evening).
- Warning: packed days leave little room for spontaneous discoveries or rest.

## Neighbourhood Compatibility

### Natural Pairings (walkable or one short train ride)
- Shibuya + Harajuku + Omotesando (walkable, same area)
- Asakusa + Ueno (Ginza Line, 5 min)
- Tsukiji + Ginza (walkable, ~15 min)
- Shinjuku + Harajuku (Yamanote Line, 2 stops)
- Roppongi + Tokyo Tower + Azabudai (walkable)

### Day Trips from Tokyo
- Kamakura (temples, Great Buddha, beach — ~1 hour by JR Yokosuka Line)
- Yokohama (Minato Mirai, Chinatown, Cup Noodles Museum — ~30 min)
- Nikko (Toshogu Shrine, nature — ~2 hours by limited express)
- Hakone (hot springs, Mount Fuji views — ~1.5 hours by Romancecar)

## Visit Duration Estimates
- Sensoji Temple + Nakamise-dori: 1.5-3 hours depending on crowd and shopping interest.
- Meiji Shrine (inner garden included): 1-2 hours.
- Shibuya Crossing + surrounding shopping: 1-3 hours.
- Tsukiji Outer Market (morning food crawl): 1.5-2 hours.
- Akihabara (browsing electronics and anime shops): 2-4 hours.
- Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden: 1-2 hours.
- teamLab Borderless: allow 2-3 hours including queue time; advance booking strongly recommended.
- Tokyo National Museum (Ueno): 2-3 hours for highlights, longer for enthusiasts.
- Roppongi Hills / Mori Art Museum: 1.5-2 hours.
