# Tokyo Practical Notes — Travel Knowledge

## Transport
- JR Yamanote Line (loop line) connects Shinjuku, Shibuya, Harajuku, Ueno, Tokyo Station, and more.
- Tokyo Metro and Toei Subway cover most central areas. A 24/48/72-hour tourist pass can be cost-effective.
- Suica or Pasmo IC cards work across nearly all trains, buses, and even at many convenience stores.
- Taxis are clean and safe but expensive. Use for short hops, late-night returns, or when carrying luggage.
- Haneda Airport (HND) is closer to the city centre (~30-40 min by train). Narita (NRT) is further (~60-90 min by Narita Express).

## Accessibility
- Major stations and newer attractions are increasingly barrier-free with elevators and ramps.
- Older neighbourhoods (Asakusa side streets, Golden Gai) may have narrow paths and stairs.
- Wheelchair-accessible taxis (Universal Design taxis) are available; book in advance or use apps.
- Many train stations have tactile paving and staff assistance available.

## Etiquette
- Walking while eating is uncommon outside market areas. Stand or sit to finish food.
- On trains, keep phone calls to a minimum and set phones to silent mode (manner mode).
- Queue orderly for trains; stand on the marked side of escalators (left in Tokyo, right in Osaka).
- Tipping is not practiced and may cause awkwardness.
- Remove shoes when entering certain restaurants, traditional accommodations, and some temples.
- Tattoos may restrict entry to some public baths, onsen, pools, and gyms.

## Check Before You Go
- Opening hours for small restaurants and shops can vary significantly and are not always updated online.
- teamLab Borderless and other popular attractions require advance online booking; walk-in availability is rare.
- Seasonal closures: some attractions close for New Year (late December to early January), and gardens may have reduced appeal in winter.
- Weather: summer (June-August) is hot and humid with occasional typhoons. Cherry blossom season (late March-early April) is peak tourist time.
- Japan Rail Pass must be purchased outside Japan before arrival (for visitors on temporary visitor status).
