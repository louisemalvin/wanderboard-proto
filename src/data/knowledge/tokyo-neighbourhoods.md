# Tokyo Neighbourhoods — Travel Knowledge

## Asakusa (Taito Ward)
- Traditional district centred on Sensoji Temple, Tokyo's oldest Buddhist temple.
- Nakamise-dori shopping street leads from Kaminarimon Gate to the temple.
- Nearby Sumida River offers river cruises and seasonal views.
- Pair naturally with: Ueno (museums, park), Ryogoku (sumo culture), or a leisurely Sumida River walk.

## Ueno (Taito Ward)
- Ueno Park houses several major museums (Tokyo National Museum, National Museum of Western Art).
- Ueno Zoo, Shinobazu Pond, and cherry blossoms in spring.
- Good neighbour pairing with Asakusa, Akihabara, or Yanaka (old Tokyo atmosphere).
- Connected to Narita Airport via Keisei Skyliner.

## Shibuya (Shibuya Ward)
- Famous for Shibuya Crossing, one of the busiest pedestrian intersections globally.
- Hachiko Statue, Shibuya Sky observation deck, and extensive shopping (Shibuya 109, Seibu, Parco).
- Connects easily to Harajuku (walkable via Cat Street), Omotesando, and Ebisu.
- Packed pace: combine with Harajuku + Shinjuku in one day with efficient train use.
- Relaxed pace: pair Shibuya + Harajuku or Shibuya + Ebisu for a more measured day.

## Harajuku (Shibuya Ward)
- Takeshita Street for youth culture, crepe shops, and quirky fashion.
- Omotesando for upscale shopping and architecture.
- Meiji Shrine, a large Shinto shrine in a forested park, provides a quiet contrast.
- Walkable from Shibuya (~20 minutes via Cat Street).

## Shinjuku (Shinjuku Ward)
- Busiest train station in the world (Shinjuku Station).
- Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden, a large landscape garden with three styles.
- Golden Gai for tiny themed bars, Omoide Yokocho for yakitori alley.
- Kabukicho entertainment district (exercise caution late at night).
- Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building offers free observation decks.

## Tsukiji (Chuo Ward)
- Former wholesale market; the outer market remains vibrant with food stalls and seafood.
- Most outer-market shops close by early afternoon — plan for morning visits.
- Nearby: Hamarikyu Gardens, Ginza (high-end shopping), Tsukiji Honganji Temple.

## Akihabara (Chiyoda Ward)
- Electronics, anime, manga, and gaming culture hub.
- Large electronics retailers (Yodobashi Camera) and countless specialist shops.
- Can be overwhelming on weekends due to pedestrian crowds.
- Pairs well with Ueno or Asakusa on the same day.

## Roppongi (Minato Ward)
- Roppongi Hills complex with Mori Art Museum, Tokyo City View observation deck.
- Nightlife and international dining.
- Nearby: Tokyo Tower, Azabudai Hills, and teamLab Borderless (Azabudai).

## Odaiba (Minato Ward)
- Artificial island in Tokyo Bay with teamLab Borderless (formerly), shopping malls, and the giant Gundam statue.
- Access via Yurikamome elevated train or Rinkai Line.
- Family-friendly with seaside parks and Oedo Onsen Monogatari.

## General Planning Notes
- Tokyo's train network is extensive and punctual. A Suica/Pasmo IC card simplifies travel.
- Peak commuter hours (roughly 7:30–9:00 and 17:30–19:00) are exceptionally crowded; avoid travelling with luggage during these times.
- Many small restaurants and shops remain cash-only. Carry yen.
- Download offline maps or a transit app (Japan Travel by Navitime, Google Maps) before arriving.
