# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Wanderboard is for travelers and trip planners who start with messy ideas, partial constraints, and scattered possibilities, then need to turn them into an editable plan. They may be planning solo, with a partner, or for a small group; their context is exploratory and iterative rather than transactional. The primary job is to create, review, refine, and save a map-first multi-day travel board without losing flexibility.

## Product Purpose

Wanderboard turns natural-language trip ideas into structured, editable travel boards with places, day assignments, cost ranges, practical notes, and itinerary previews. It exists to make trip planning feel organized without becoming rigid: AI helps create and adjust the board, while the user remains in control of the plan. Success means users can quickly move from an uncertain prompt to a trustworthy planning canvas they can edit, revisit, and refine.

## Brand Personality

Polished, simple, elegant. The product should feel calm, precise, and quietly capable, with the workflow confidence of tools like Figma or Notion. The voice should be clear and helpful, avoiding hype while making complex planning steps feel manageable.

## Anti-references

Avoid over-decorated AI/SaaS clichés, heavy gradients, default glassmorphism, travel-agency brochure aesthetics, noisy map UI, and novelty that competes with the planning task. Wanderboard should not feel like a static itinerary generator, a destination content site, or a decorative demo. Do not hardcode a destination-first identity; the app should adapt to the trip the user describes.

## Design Principles

- Keep the board editable: every generated plan should feel like a starting point the user can shape.
- Let the map and itinerary share context without fighting for attention.
- Prefer familiar, trustworthy product patterns over ornamental invention.
- Make uncertainty explicit with assumptions, warnings, and check-before-you-go notes.
- Preserve calm focus: reduce clutter, keep actions obvious, and make recovery paths easy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use WCAG AA as the baseline for contrast, focus visibility, keyboard access, and readable text. Controls should remain operable without relying on color alone, and map-adjacent information should be available through panels or text summaries. Motion should support reduced-motion preferences and should communicate state rather than decoration. Error, empty, unavailable-AI, and local-storage failure states should provide clear recovery actions.
